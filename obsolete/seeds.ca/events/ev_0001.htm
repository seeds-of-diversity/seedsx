Seedy Events January - July 2000

This is a list of events of interest to members of Seeds of Diversity and others interested in plant biodiversity, heritage gardening, organic gardening, and seeds. If you know of any other upcoming events, please send the information to webmaster@seeds.ca.

Celebrate Surrey's Garden Heritage at Historic Stewart Farm

Surrey BC, April 15 2000

Surrey's citizens have been gardening since the first settlers arrived before the turn of the last century, growing fruits and vegetables for their tables, to sell at market and to feed their livestock, herbs to treat minor illnesses, and flowers to lift weary spirits. On Saturday, April 15 learn more about Surrey's gardening traditions by visiting the Historic Stewart Farm where you can shop the Heritage Plant and Seed Sale put on by the Heritage Garden Volunteers, and take a tour of this historic site's heirloom vegetable and flower gardens. From 1 to 3 pm, join garden writer Jeff Kingsley for an illustrated presentation on heritage seeds. A restored 1894 farmhouse and heritage site, the Historic Stewart Farm is located at 13723 Crescent Road, Surrey, B.C. For more information and to pre-register for the presentation, call 604 502-6456.

A Native Plant Garden in the City

Vancouver BC, Thursday May 4 2000

One of the Cedar Lecture Series, this is a presentation by Frank and Erin Skelton. Nestled in the heart of Vancouver's Dunbar area is an intriguing native plant garden developed by Frank and Erin Skelton over the past 20 years. Their garden has over 100 species of native plants, including 14 species of native ferns and a sphagnum hummock with wild cranberries and our native insect-eating sundew plant. This fascinating garden has been featured on two national television gardening programs and is included in Lorraine Johnson's book Grow Wild.

Frank recently retired from the Pacific Agriculture Research Center and is current director of the Native Plant Society of B.C. and chairman of the native plant section of the VanDusen plant sale. Erin is an avid gardener, amateur botanist, and birder. For more information, call 604 878-9821 or e-mail educate@vandusen.org.

The Business of Seeds

Edmonton AB, February 12 and 13 2000

This NAIT Continuing Education course taught by Katie Benschop, PAg, covers the basics of seed production for a number of crop specialties, including ornamental flowers and grains, culinary and medicinal herbs, and native plants. There will be a discussion of cultivation practices, harvesting, processing, and marketing these alternative crops. Hands-on laboratory classes will cover plant anatomy and taxonomy, seed cleaning methods, and packaging. There will also be a session on the business practices required to manage this new type of enterprise. Blooming Prairie, 9535 76 Avenue, Edmonton. Call 780 471-6248 to register. For more information, visit the Blooming Prairie Web site at http://www.grower.com.
