Seedy Events September 1998 - January 1999

This is a list of events of interest to members of Seeds of Diversity and others interested in plant biodiversity, heritage gardening, organic gardening, and seeds. If you know of any other upcoming events, please send the information to webmaster@seeds.ca.

Cultivating Canadian Gardens

Ottawa ON, February 17 to December 13 1998 from 9 am to 10 pm daily

An exhibition devoted to the history of gardening in Canada opens at the National Library of Canada. The exhibition is based on more than 175 books, periodicals, photographs, and other memorabilia that trace Canada's gardening history from 17th century records of "the three sisters" - the Huron gardens of corn, beans, and squash - to the proliferation of books and magazines of the last decade.

The History of Gardening in Canada exhibition is on at the National Library of Canada at 395 Wellington Street, Ottawa.

Consider the Lilies of the Field: Victorian Lessons of the Natural World

Milton ON, exhibited until early 1999

The Halton Region Museum has mounted a display exploring Victorian attitudes to nature from earliest settlement times to the turn of the century. The exhibit includes outstanding examples from the 19th century horticultural collection of Milton collector Craig Gamache, as well as rarely seen items from the Halton Region Museum's artifact collection. Included in the exhibition is a special section devoted to Isabella Preston, former Georgetown resident and first professional woman plant hybridist in Canada, best known for her work with lilies. The Halton Region Museum is located 7 km west of Milton, Ontario in Kelso Conservation Area on Regional Road 28.

The Cedar Lecture Series

Vancouver BC, Tuesdays November 3 and December 1 1998, at 8pm

VanDusen Botanical Garden in Vancouver presents a series of lectures at the Floral Hall at 8 pm.

Among the Giant Trees is the topic on Tuesday, November 3. What live for centuries and are among the largest life forms on our planet? The colossal trees of the West Coast, one of the earth's greatest treasures. Audrey Grescoe, a failed gardener who moved to Bowen Island and discovered the marvels of the forest, which she doesn't have to weed, will charm you with the stories of these giants their histories, legends, uses, and abuses. Find out more about forest friends and foes, such as wildfires and insects. Discover the mysteries of tree rings and where the West Coast's biggest trees can be found! Photographer Bob Herger's magnificent slides portray these giants in all their moods and majesty.

Greenways and Public Ways in the City of Gardens is the topic on Tuesday, December 1. Streets and public open space, as a healthy percentage of our public realm, need to perform much better as general contributors for a more livable urban landscape. Moura Quayle, Dean of the Faculty of Agricultural Sciences at UBC and former Director of their Landscape Architecture Program, relates urban greenways and public ways to ecological sustainability, positive economic implications, and healthy, beautiful communities, with illustrated local and international examples.

For more information, contact Lorri Espeseth, Garden Course Co-ordinator, VanDusen Botanical Garden, 5251 Oak Street, Vancouver, BC V6M 4H1; e-mail educate@vandusen.org.

Courses at the Royal Botanical Gardens

Hamilton ON, October 13, 17, Nov 1, 5, 12, 19, 26 1998

Feng Shui in the Garden: The Pathway to Enlightenment is offered on Tuesday, October 13, 7 to 9 pm. This course will introduce ways to enhance your environment with the use of plants and related material. You can create harmony, balance, and comfort by using the 3,000-year-old Chinese art of design, location, and arrangement. You'll learn about natural cures for bad spaces, including water features, colours, shapes, and the ancient wisdom of nature's elements. Begin to understand gardens as an expression of self. Instructor Kathryn Potter is a Feng Shui consultant, designer, and teacher.

On Saturday, October 17, 9 am to 4 pm, learn about Naturalization: A Carolinian Life Zone Approach. Offered in conjunction with the Arboretum at the University of Guelph, this workshop is for gardeners, landowners, teachers, and parents interested in taking an environmentally friendly approach to their landscape, by naturalizing their property or schoolyard. The instructors Chris Earley, Interpretive Naturalist, and Henry Kock, Interpretive Horticulturist, are both with the Arboretum.

Sunday, November 1, 1:30 to 4:30 pm, learn about Herbs for your Pet. Find out about useful house-hold herbs that could also benefit your pet in this discussion and question-and-answer session with Dr. Sharon Kopinak, a Rockwood veterinarian who uses alternative therapies.

Botany for Beginners is offered on Thursdays, November 5 to 26, 7 to 9 pm. This course will give you a guided tour of the plant kingdom and help you to decipher your gardening and wildflower books. In-depth workshops through the winter follow up on the material introduced in this course by Tyler Smith, RBG's field botanist and a former provincial park interpreter.

For more information, contact Barbara McKean, Public Programs Co-ordinator, Royal Botanical Gardens, PO Box 399, Hamilton, ON L8N 3H8; telephone 905 527-1158 ext 247; fax 905 577-0375; e-mail bmckean@rbg.ca.

Making the Transition to Organic Farming

Guelph ON, January 29-31 1999

The 18th Annual Organic Conference & Eco-Products Trade Show takes place January 29-31, 1999 at Guelph University.

Friday afternoon features two intensive workshops -- "Establishing a Community Shared Agriculture Project as an Alternative Social and Economic Model" and "Soil Fertility Planning for an Organic Farming System" -- and the "Meet the Presenters Fundraiser", featuring organic food and wine, presenting the public with an opportunity to meet the organic movers and shakers of this expanding agricultural sector.

Joel Salatin, author of "Pastured Poultry Profits" and "Salad Bar Beef" will give the Saturday morning keynote talk on "Creating the Farm Your Children Will Want".

Saturday features 20 workshops, designed to satisfy the requests and preferences of over 550 evaluations from the 1998 event. Workshops are grouped under these major themes -- crops, livestock, marketing; how to grow organically; greenhouses, biodynamics, permaculture, medicinals; and community shared agriculture.

The conference ends on Sunday with a full-day program on biodynamics.

For more information, contact Tomas Nimmo, Conference Co-ordinator, PO Box 116, Collingwood, ON L9Y 3Z4; phone 705 444-0923; fax 705 444-0380; e-mail organix@georgian.net; or check out the Organic Conference Web site at http://www.gks.com/OrgConf/.
