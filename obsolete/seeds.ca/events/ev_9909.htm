Seedy Events October 1999 - February 2000

This is a list of events of interest to members of Seeds of Diversity and others interested in plant biodiversity, heritage gardening, organic gardening, and seeds. If you know of any other upcoming events, please send the information to webmaster@seeds.ca.

Cedar Lecture at VanDusen Botanical Garden

Vancouver BC, Nov 4 1999

On Thursday, November 4, join Hugh Daubeny on an exploration of Exotics from Down Under, unusual native plants found in Australia and New Zealand. This entertaining slide presentation will take you on a tour of six botanical gardens, where you will meet such intriguing residents as bottle brush, toe toe, and Polutukawa, the New Zealand Christmas tree. Recently retired from the Pacific Agriculture Research Centre, Hugh is a fellow of the American Society for Horticultural Science. For more information, contact Garden Course Co-ordinator, VanDusen Botanical Garden, 5251 Oak Street, Vancouver, BC V6M 4H1; e-mail educate@vandusen.org.

Growing the Organic Market

Guelph ON, Jan 27-30 2000

The 19th Annual Organic Conference & Eco-Products Trade Show is being held on January 27 to 30, 2000 at the University of Guelph. Eliot Coleman, author of several books and expert market gardener, will deliver the Saturday keynote on ‘Organic Market Opportunities for the Next 10 Years' and take part in several other presentations. Barbara Damrosch, author of several books and TV gardening personality, will participate in workshops. About 35 speakers will present on introduction to ecological agriculture, harvest management, organic soil fertility, organic farming and fair trade and social justice. The Eco-Products Trade Show in the University Centre runs on Saturday and Sunday.

For more information, contact Tomas Nimmo, PO Box 116, Collingwood, ON L9Y 3Z4; e-mail organix@georgian.net; or visit http://www.gks.com/OrgConf/

Starting a Heritage Vegetable Garden

Toronto ON, Feb 10 2000, 6:30-9:30pm

Using seeds passed along by generations of gardeners, learn about the origins of foods we eat and how to plan your garden, in your yard, on your balcony, or on your roof. the event takes place at the Canadian College of Naturopathic Medicine, 1255 Sheppard E., Toronto. To register, call 416 498-1255 ext 233.

Seeds of Diversity AGM

Montreal QC, Nov 14 1999, 1:00pm

Seeds of Diversity Canada is holding its fourth Annual General Meeting at 1:00 pm on Sunday, November 14, 1999 in Montreal, Quebec. This year, members attending the AGM will have the opportunity to exchange seeds and hear our two guest speakers. Yves Gagnon, prominent author and horticulturist, who appears weekly on national CBC television's "La semaine verte", will speak on "La production domestique de semences de légumes" (Domestic production of vegetable seeds). Bob Wildfong, president of SoDC, will also give a talk on seed sowing techniques. We invite all members to attend this meeting.

The AGM will take place at Université du Québec à Montréal in conference room RR130, Pavillon Sciences de la Gestion, 315 Ste-Catherine Street East. It is conveniently located in the centre of Montreal and is close to the Berri-UQAM Metro station.

Programme Semencier du Patrimoine assemblée annuelle

Montreal QC, Nov 14 1999, 1:00pm

Programme Semencier du Patrimoine tiendra sa quatrième assemblée annuelle le dimanche à 13:00 heures le 14 novembre 1999 à Montréal, Québec. Cette année, en plus de l'agenda normal, nous offrons l'opportunité d'un échange de semences entre les membres présents ainsi que ces conférenciers. Yves Gagnon, auteur réputé, horticulteur et chroniqueur spécialisé à l'émission “ La semaine verte ” diffusée d'un océan à l'autre sur les ondes de Radio-Canada, nous présentera la conférence "La production domestique de semences de légumes". Bob Wildfong, président de Programme Semencier du Patrimoine, nous parlera aussi des différentes techniques de récolte de semences. Nous invitons tous les membres à assister à cette rencontre.

Cette rencontre se tiendra à Université du Québec à Montréal, salle de conférence RR130, Pavillon Sciences de la Gestion, 315, rue Ste-Catherine est. L'endroit est facilement accessible et près de la station de Métro Berri-UQAM.

Garden Talks at the Fredericton Botanic Garden

Fredericton NB, Oct 28 1999, 7pm

The Fredericton Botanic Garden Association begins this season's series of informative garden talks on October 28 at 7 pm at the Garden Resource Center on Cameron Court off Hanwell Road.

The first speaker this season will be Daryl Hunter, a member of North American Fruit Explorers (NAFEX), from Keswick Ridge, N.B. His hobby of 20 years has been apples, of which he is a collector of historic and modern varieties - 135 - in fact. His focus is on disease resistance in both historic and modern varieties, and he is just beginning to work with Kings Landing on a project to re-establish their orchards. His presentation to us will include a slide presentation.
