Seedy Events December 2001 - February 2002

This is a list of events of interest to members of Seeds of Diversity and others interested in plant biodiversity, heritage gardening, organic gardening, and seeds. If you know of any other upcoming events, please send the information to webmaster@seeds.ca.

This Land is Our Land

Vancouver BC, Thursday December 6 2001

Part of the Cedar Lecture Series, Linda and Larry Marshik will be speaking at VanDusen Botanic Gardens. In July, 1999, the Marshiks embarked on a six-month journey of discovery across Canada and the US. Their slide presentation will illustrate the geology, plants, and wildlife of our vast continent and include examples from VanDusen's plant collections. They will explore interconnections between natural landscapes and human heritage in the home between two oceans that we all share. For more information, call 604 878-9821 or e-mail educate@vandusen.org.

24 Days of Winter Peace in a Chinese Garden

Vancouver BC, December 1 to 24 2002, 10am-4:30pm

Visit the Dr. Sun Yat-Sen Classical Chinese Garden in Vancouver between December 1 and 24 to enjoy some winter peace. This garden is one of Vancouver's living treasures. In the midst of Chinatown, lies a tranquil yet vibrant expression of Ming Dynasty culture. Within this earthly paradise, the garden offers regularly scheduled guided tours, which provide interesting perspectives on Chinese culture, life during the Ming Dynasty, architecture, and horticulture.

For more information, contact Erika Korstrom at the Dr. Sun Yat Sen Garden Society of Vancouver, 578 Carral Street, Vancouver V6B 5K2; phone 604-662-3207; fax 604-682-4008; sunyatsen@telus.net; or visit http://www.vancouverchinesegarden.com.

Organic Agriculture and the Farm Economy

Guelph ON, January 25 to 27 2002

The 21st Annual Organic Conference and Eco-Products Trade Show takes place at the University of Guelph, on January 25-27, 2002. This year's keynote speaker is Gunnar Rundgren, president of the International Federation of Organic Agriculture Movements and a leading consultant in organic certification in Sweden. He will be talking on January 26 on "Organics/Europe - Lessons For North American Agriculture".

On January 25 will be the public forum panel on "Can Organics Save the Farm Economy?" Work-shop topics include BioDynamics, organic soil fertility, city-based organic food distribution, organic processing, Canada's native food wisdom, marketing, profitable market gardening, livestock health, organic product development, draft horses in organic farming, and apprenticeships. This three-day conference kicks off with a special introductory two-day training course and will also feature four intensive three-hour training seminars and expert panels.

The Organic Trade Show in the University Centre runs January 26-27 and is free to all visitors. Both large- and small-scale exhibitors come from all over Canada and the northern US to market products and services. For information, e-mail organix@georgian.net or visit http://www.organicfarms.ca.
