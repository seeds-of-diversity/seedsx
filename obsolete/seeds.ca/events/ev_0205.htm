People and Place: Making Connections

Sydney NS, June 23-28 2002

ALHFAM, The Association for Living History, Farm and Agricultural Museums, is holding its 2002 Annual Meeting and Conference June 23 to 28 at the Fortress of Louisbourg in Cape Breton, Nova Scotia. Cape Bretoners are known for the strong and enduring connections they share with one another and the place they call home. That powerful sense of connection and continuity is expressed through a rich mosaic of tradition, songs, stories, dance, literature, arts, crafts, and celebrations. Conveying connections among people and the places they call home breathes life into our museums and sites. Through research, preservation and interpretation how do we enable visitors to understand and feel those connections? How do we accurately and responsibly reveal both their positive and negative aspects, and avoid creating myths?

For more information, contact Anita Campbell, Parks Canada, 1869 Upper Water Street, Historic Properties, Halifax, NS B3J 1S9; anita.campbell@pch.gc.ca

Cultivating Communities

Victoria BC, August 21-28 2002

Canadian Organic Growers is organizing the 2002 Organic World Congress on behalf of the International Federation of Organic Agriculture Movements (IFOAM). The main events are the 14th Organic World Congress, the 4th IFOAM Organic World Exhibition, the 7th International Congress on Viticulture and Wine, and the IFOAM General Assembly. The congress takes place August 21-28 in Victoria, BC. The Organic World Congress will bring together representatives of the organic movement from around the world. It is open to everyone interested in organic agriculture and sustainable development: farmers, researchers, advisors, food processors, traders, certifiers, policy makers, and consumers. Over 1000 delegates are expected to attend.

For more information, contact IFOAM 2002, c/o Bldg. 20, 8801 East Saanich Road, Sidney, BC V8L 1H3; telephone 250 655 5652; fax 250 655-5657; e-mail ifoam2002@cog.ca, or visit the Web site http://www.cog.ca/ifoam2002.

Harvest Festival

Lotbinière QC, 27-29 September 2002

Celebrate a Harvest Festival at Domaine Joly de Lotbiniere, PQ (near Quebec City). One of the most beautiful gardens in Quebec offers visitors an exceptional encounter with history and nature; a stroll beneath the century-old trees, a garden of a thousand colours and scents, a walk through the forest and an adventure at the river. The harvest festival will take place between September 27-29. The weekdays are for school groups whereas the entire family is invited for the weekend. Workshops, contests, an educational rally, a treasure hunt as well as demonstrations and samplings of rare vegetables and fruits will be offered to visitors.

For more information, contact Karine Lévesque at 418 926-2462, by fax at 418 926-2525, or by e-mail at domjoly@globetrotter.net.

Le Festival des Recoltes

Lotbinière QC, 27-29 septembre 2002

Le Domaine Joly-De Lotbinière est reconnu comme l'un des plus beaux jardins du Quebec. En 1998, la Fondation du Domaine Joly-De Lotbinière en est devenue proprietaire afin d'assurer la perennité de ce site patrimonial unique au benefice des generations presentes et futures. Cette oasis de paix et de beauté offre donc a tous ses visiteurs une rencontre exceptionnelle avec l'histoire et la nature, une balade sous les arbres centenaires, un jardin aux mille couleurs et parfums, une marche en foret et une aventure au fleuve. Cette annee, un evenement automnal s'ajoutera a toutes nos activites d'education et de sensibilisation, c'est le Festival des Recoltes. Cette fête se deroulera du 27 au 29 septembre. Par ce Festival, nous desirons creer une activite familiale d'education et de sensibilisation. Des ateliers, des concours, un rallye educatif, une chasse aux tresors ainsi que des demonstrations et des degustations de fruits et de legumes rares seront offertes aux visiteurs.

Si vous avez des questions, veuillez contacter Karine Lévesque au 418 926-2462; ou domjoly@globetrotter.net.
