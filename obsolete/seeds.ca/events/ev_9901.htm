Seedy Events January - May 1999

This is a list of events of interest to members of Seeds of Diversity and others interested in plant biodiversity, heritage gardening, organic gardening, and seeds. If you know of any other upcoming events, please send the information to webmaster@seeds.ca.

Our Food, Our Health, Our Future

Winnipeg MN, February 26-27 1999

The Manitoba Eco-Network, Organic Producers Association of Manitoba, University of Winnipeg Students' Association, Metis Heritage and Horticulture Society, Winnipeg Vegetarian Association, Manitoba Agriculture, EJLB Foundation, and Environment Canada are sponsoring a Conference on Organic Agriculture in Manitoba at the University of Winnipeg.

On Friday evening, Lynn Miller, editor of the Small Farmer's Journal, gives the keynote speech on "Where is your next meal coming from?" (Friday evening admission is $5)

The Saturday morning plenary session, on "can organic farmers feed the world?", is followed by an organic luncheon provided by local producers, at which Pat Mooney, Executive Director of Rural Advancement Foundation International, will talk on "The privatization of nature".

Saturday afternoon sessions are on the growing market for organics; heritage seeds and seed exchange; developments in biotechnology; the politics and policies of organic certification; chemical-free weed and insect control; pesticide drift and transfer; where are young organic farmers coming from; and social/health/environment benefits. (Admission for Saturday, including luncheon, is $30 or $20 for low income).

For additional information about the conference or to obtain a trade show booth or to register, call Manitoba Eco-Network at 204 947-6511, fax 204 947-6514, e-mail men@web.net, or mail #2 - 70 Albert Street, Winnipeg, MB R3B 1E7.

Green Thumbs

Toronto ON, January 31, February 7, 14, 21, and 28 1999

Come to Heritage Toronto (Toronto Historical Board) Spadina House on Sundays for a series of how-to lectures of interest to green thumbs, from beginners to the experienced. Topics will cover a wide range of gardening subjects of interest to both city and country gardeners. The talks will be held on from 1:00 to 3:30 pm, at a cost of $15 a lecture. For more information, call 416 392-6910.

Workshops for the Gardener

Hamilton ON, February 17, 27, March 3, 6, 24, 31, April 7, 14 1999

These are a few of the many workshops that the Royal Botanical Gardens in Hamilton, Ontario is hosting over the first half of 1999.

  • Grafting for Beginners on Wednesday, February 17, 7 to 9 pm provides the opportunity to practise the art of grafting using coloured dogwoods. Some flowering crabapple, juniper, and ash understock will be provided, along with handouts to help you use grafting to save money, "build" your own weeping trees, or save a family heirloom plant, and, of course, most of all to have fun.
  • Native Plants for the Natural Landscape on Saturday, February 27, 10 am to 4 pm takes a look at native plants from a Native perspective, including a multimedia presentation, information on seeding, propagating and transplanting native plants followed by a garden design project. This workshop will focus on sun-loving and prairie plants.
  • Starting Plants from Seed on Wednesday, March 3, 7:30 to 10 pm covers the most effective techniques for germinating seed for annuals, perennials, and trees, just in time to get your vegetables and flowers underway for this summer. You will receive at least 4 packets of seeds, as well as a tray and growing media. Limited woody plant seeds will also be available.
  • The Environmental Gardener on Saturday, March 6, 10 am to 3 pm will help you take a more environmentally friendly approach to managing your home landscape. Topics covered will include pest management without chemicals, water-wise gardening, mulching, composting, natural fertilizing, and ways to welcome wildlife into your garden.
  • Biodiversity Basics: Taxonomy, Ecology, and Conservation in the Plant Kingdom on Wednesdays, March 24 to April 14, 7 to 9 pm will introduce you to the science behind the buzz-words. Examining the related fields of taxonomy, ecology, and conservation biology, we'll take an informed look at current biodiversity issues in southern Ontario.
  • The Gardening with Kids workshop on Wednesday, April 14, 7 to 9:30 pm is meant for teachers, parents, camp leaders, or anyone who wants to involve children in growing and nurturing plants indoors and out. We'll cover ways to keep their interest whetted, meaningful gardening projects, fun garden games, disaster-proof planting projects, and more.

For registration information, contact the RBG at Course Registration, Royal Botanical Gardens, PO Box 399, Hamilton, ON L8N 3H8 or call 905 527-1158 and ask for the Registration Desk.

Restoring a Garden

Toronto ON, ongoing

Springtime brings beauty and activity to the gardens at Colborne Lodge in High Park in Toronto, as restoration of the historic gardens is now underway. In May, seedlings grow in the glasshouse, and garden beds are prepared for planting. Visitors touring the Lodge can learn about traditional plants and practices. Free with admission.

Wildflower and Plant Sale

Toronto ON, Sunday May 9 1999, 10 am to 2 pm

The North American Native Plant Society, formerly the Canadian Wildflower Society, is holding their annual sale of wildflowers and plants once again at the Civic Garden Centre in Toronto. Come early to get the best selection of plants and seeds from native wildflowers, trees, and shrubs.

Heritage Plant Sale at Doon

Kitchener ON, Saturday May 22 1999, 10:00 am to 4:30 pm

Get a chance to grow a 100-year-old tomato when the Friends of Doon hold their fourth annual plant sale this spring. On sale will be heritage annuals, perennials, vegetables, and herbs, all varieties that are grown in the gardens of the recreated historic village; the same varieties that flourished in Waterloo County at the beginning of the 20th century. Plant a piece of history this year in your garden by purchasing a heritage plant at Doon. All proceeds from the sale go to the Friends of Doon. For more information, call 519 748-1914.

Celebration of Spring

Edmonton AB, Monday May 24 1999, 10:00 am to 6:00 pm

Experience the good old days through historic demonstrations and spring-time activities at the Ukrainian Cultural Heritage Village. See traditional spring field work and antique farm machinery in action. A special feature of the day is the Ukrainian Dance Extravaganza where over 200 dancers from the local area will entertain you.

The Village is 25 minutes east of Edmonton on Highway 16, just east of Elk Island National Park. For more information, call 780 662-3640 or email ofowler@mcd.gov.ab.ca.
